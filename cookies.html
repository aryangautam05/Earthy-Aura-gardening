Cookie Policy

Learn how Earthy Aura Gardening uses cookies to enhance your experience and protect your privacy.

Introduction

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work better, remember your preferences, and provide analytics.

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, what types we use, and your choices regarding cookies while using the Earthy Aura Gardening website.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are tiny files stored in your browser or device that help websites recognize you and remember your actions or preferences over time.

Cookies can be 'session' cookies that expire when you close your browser or 'persistent' cookies that remain until expiry or deletion.

Cookies serve many purposes: authenticating users, maintaining session states, personalizing content, and understanding user behavior to improve websites.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Recognize your device on return visits
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our website to improve it
  • Enable secure user authentication and protect your account
  • Deliver relevant marketing and promotions (with your consent)
  • Ensure functionality like shopping carts and forms operate smoothly

Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable security features, navigation, and access to secure areas.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting anonymous data about page visits, time spent, and navigation patterns.

Functionality Cookies

Used to remember choices you make and provide enhanced features such as personalized greetings or saved preferences.

Targeting and Advertising Cookies

These cookies deliver ads relevant to your interests and measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns. We only use these with your consent.

Technical Details: Understanding Cookie Types and Security

Cookies are set by web servers via HTTP headers or by JavaScript running in your browser. They include various attributes controlling their behavior and security, such as:

  • Session vs Persistent: Session cookies last only while your browser is open; persistent cookies remain stored until expiry or manual deletion.
  • Secure: Cookies marked as Secure are only transmitted via HTTPS connections, protecting them from interception.
  • HttpOnly: This flag prevents JavaScript access to cookies, mitigating some cross-site scripting risks.
  • SameSite: Controls whether cookies are sent with cross-site requests, helping prevent cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks.
  • Domain and Path: Define the scope of where a cookie is sent in subsequent requests.

Lifecycle of a Cookie

When you visit a website, cookies are created and stored on your device. Browsers send relevant cookies back to the server with each request matching domain and path rules. Cookies expire either via a set expiry date or when the session ends.

Cookies can be manually deleted by users or automatically cleared depending on browser settings or privacy modes.

First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by the domain you are visiting and typically used for site functionality and personalization. Third-party cookies come from external sites embedded in the page, often used for analytics or advertising.

Many browsers restrict third-party cookie usage to protect user privacy, and some tracking protections block them entirely by default.

Legal Compliance and Cookies

Cookies are subject to various data protection laws worldwide, such as:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation): Requires informed consent for non-essential cookies in the EU.
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act): Grants consumers rights regarding their data including opt-out from sale of personal information.
  • ePrivacy Directive: Requires cookie consent disclosures in the EU.
  • Other national laws: Many countries have cookie-related legislation with varying levels of compliance requirements.

Our cookie consent dialog is designed to meet these legal requirements by allowing visitors to accept or decline non-essential cookies.

Consent Management

Obtaining and managing user consent accurately is vital. Our site:

  • Provides clear information on cookie usage
  • Allows users to accept or decline non-essential cookies
  • Stores and respects your choice via your browser localStorage
  • Does not deploy tracking cookies without consent

Managing Cookies on Your Browser

Each browser has mechanisms to manage cookie settings:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies and site data

Adjusting these settings can block, delete, or restrict cookies based on your preferences.

Emerging Technologies and The Future of Cookies

With increasing privacy concerns, new standards and technologies are emerging:

  • SameSite Attribute: Increasingly enforced by browsers to restrict cross-site cookie sharing.
  • Browser privacy features: Tracking prevention and fingerprinting resistance reduce cookie availability.
  • Privacy Sandbox: Google’s initiative to replace third-party cookies with privacy-preserving technologies.
  • Local Storage and IndexedDB: Alternative client-side data storage mechanisms.
  • Server-Side Tracking: Moving analytics to backend to enhance privacy.

Security Considerations

Cookies can be targets for attacks such as:

  • Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Mitigated by HttpOnly flags.
  • Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF): Mitigated by SameSite attributes and other anti-CSRF tokens.
  • Session Hijacking: Encryption, secure cookies, and secure HTTPS prevent interception.

We implement best practices in our cookie handling to protect your information.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I decline cookies?

If you decline non-essential cookies, some features of the site may be limited or unavailable, but your privacy will be respected.

Can I change my cookie preferences later?

Yes, you can clear cookies in your browser or revisit our site to update your consent through the cookie dialog if presented again.

Do cookies track me across all sites?

Third-party cookies track behavior across multiple sites, while first-party cookies only remember info on the current site. We only set third-party cookies with your consent.

Are cookies safe?

Cookies themselves are safe text files and cannot carry viruses, but misuse and tracking without consent can be privacy concerns, which we address with transparency.

Why can't I use some site features if I block cookies?

Many websites rely on cookies for essential functions such as login sessions, language preferences, or shopping carts that require cookies to remember your actions.
